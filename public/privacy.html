Pulse — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 15 June 2026

Pulse is a personal wellness tracker created by Shreyaan Datta. This policy explains what data Pulse handles and how. The guiding principle is simple: your wellness data is yours. Pulse does not sell your data, does not show ads, and does not use your logs for advertising or profiling.

What Pulse stores

By default, everything you log — water, workouts, meals, sleep, mood, steps, weight and any notes — is stored locally on your own device, in your browser/app storage. It never leaves your device unless you choose to create a cloud account (below).

Optional cloud account

If you choose to create an account, Pulse uses Supabase to sync your data across your devices and to power optional social features (Friends and Family, which you opt into per connection). In that case we store:

  • Your email address (for sign-in and account recovery).
  • Your wellness data, in a private row that is access-controlled to your account only.
  • For Friends/Family you opt into: a curated daily summary you explicitly choose to share. Your journal/mood notes are never shared.

Using a cloud account is entirely optional — Pulse works fully as a local, on-device app without one.

Optional reminders

If you enable reminders, Pulse stores a push-notification token and your time zone so it can send you a daily "keep your streak alive" nudge. You can turn this off at any time in Settings, which removes the token.

Importing health data

If you use the "Sync health data" feature, the file you export from Apple Health or Google Fit is read entirely on your device to import steps, sleep, workouts and weight. The file is not uploaded anywhere.

Payments

The optional Pulse Plus subscription is processed by Razorpay. Pulse never sees or stores your card details — those are handled by Razorpay under their own privacy policy.

What Pulse does not do

  • No advertising and no ad networks.
  • No selling or renting of your personal data.
  • No third-party analytics that track you across other apps or sites.

Deleting your data

On-device data is removed when you reset Pulse or clear app storage. If you have a cloud account and want it deleted, email the address below and your account and its data will be removed.
